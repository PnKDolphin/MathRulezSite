Monomials

Adding

Subtracting

2x + 5x = 2+5x

2x - 5x = 2-5x

Adding monomials is simple, add the coefficients of your like term while keeping the variables and coefficients exactly the same!

Remember, "like terms" are terms with identical variables and exponents.

Similarly to the addition of monomials, subtraction is all about collecting you like terms and combining them. In this case, we must subtract their coefficients and leave the variables and exponents alone

Example

3xy + 5xy + 2y = 8xy + 2y

Example

6t - 3t - 7t² = 3t - 7t²

Product         and         Quotient

Rules

xᵃ ∘ xᵇ = xᵃ*ᵇ

xᵃ / xᵇ = xᵃ⁻ᵇ

Multiplying like term variables with exponents can look tricky at first. Especially expressions like 2³∘2⁴, which deals with larger exponent numbers. However, when written out as 2∘2∘2∘2∘2∘2∘2, or 2⁷, we learn that a much simpler way of thinking about these complex operations is to simply add the exponents together. Remember to watch for hidden 'ones' in your exponents.

Dividing like term variables with exponents sounds like it would be even bigger of a challenge than multiplication. However, it actually follows a very similar principle! When running our operation, instead of adding our exponents, we instead subtract them. For example, 3⁵ / 3² would be equivalent to 3⁵⁻², or 3³.

3xy + 5xy + 2y = 8xy + 2y

6t - 3t - 7t² = 3t - 7t²

The Power Rule

(xᵃ)ᵇ = xᵃ*ᵇ

Applying exponents to terms that already have exponents (see example below) although intimidating, is quite elementary. To find our new exponent, we simply need to multiply our current exponents together to find our new one!

(2⁴)² = 2⁴*² = 2⁸
